Who I am

About

I am a Master of Engineering student at Saga University, Japan (GPA 3.9/4.0), studying Computer Science and Information Technology under Professor Eisuke Hanada. My research focuses on RFID-based Digital Twin simulation for emergency department resource management.

What drives my work is a practical problem: Bangladesh's public hospitals operate at 3–4 times capacity, yet deploying RFID without prior evidence is expensive and risky. I build simulation tools that generate that evidence cheaply, before any hardware is purchased.

I am actively seeking a fully funded PhD position in healthcare informatics, biomedical engineering, or IoT systems — primarily targeting Australia (RTP) and international programs. Thesis defense: July 14, 2026.

Simulation Systems

Two integrated systems form the thesis: a MATLAB 2D agent-based simulation (BASE_SYSTEM V5.3 / ADMIN_SYSTEM V5.5) and a Unity 3D Digital Twin layer. Together they validate RFID-IoT nearest-nurse dispatch across 50 stochastic runs from 39 to 762 patient arrivals.
