Who I am

About

I am a Master of Engineering student at Saga University, Japan (GPA 3.9/4.0), conducting research on RFID-based Digital Twin systems for emergency department resource management, supervised by Professor Eisuke Hanada.

My thesis develops simulation-based evidence for RFID-IoT deployment in resource-constrained hospitals — validated across 50 stochastic runs covering 39 to 762 patient arrivals, demonstrating 99.7% alert resolution and cross-census robustness from quiet overnight periods to mass casualty surge.

I am actively seeking a fully funded PhD position in healthcare informatics, biomedical engineering, or IoT systems — with primary focus on Australia (RTP) and international programs. Thesis defense: July 14, 2026.

Simulation Systems

Two integrated systems form the thesis contribution — a MATLAB 2D agent-based simulation (BASE_SYSTEM V5.3 / ADMIN_SYSTEM V5.5) and a Unity 3D Digital Twin visualization layer. Together they validate RFID-IoT dispatch across 50 stochastic runs covering 39 to 762 patient arrivals.
